Sample Engagement

Selling Flood Risk: Mapping hidden financial exposure in NYC home sales

A research question about whether NYC homebuyers were unknowingly purchasing into flood zones became a parcel-level spatial analysis that changed state law.

"How many NYC homebuyers are unknowingly purchasing properties in projected flood zones — and what's the financial exposure?"

What We Delivered

We analyzed every one-to-three-family home sale in 2023, cross-referencing parcel data with projected 2050 storm surge and stormwater flood zones.

The spatial analysis revealed that nearly 1 in 5 homes sold citywide were at high flood risk, with concentrated vulnerability in Staten Island (31% of sales, $703M) and Brooklyn (26%, $1.38B).

The interactive maps and data made visible a systemic risk transfer: buyers, lenders, and insurers were absorbing flood risk without adequate disclosure.

Deliverables
